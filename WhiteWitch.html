About

This is the White Witch moth, one of the largest moths in this world. They are also known for their scientific name Thysania agrippina. A couple of other common names are the Ghost moth, great grey witch, or owlet moth. The wingspan of these great insects is about 30cm or 12 inches. Even though the atlas moth has a larger wingspan, these moths are still larger than them. Typically, the females are generally bigger than the males, and in this case, they are again like most moths. These moths use their colors to hide within the trees because they closely resemble tree bark, so this allows them to be able to hide during the day from many predators.

Cultural Significants

The spirituality of this moth comes a lot from its color because white is usually connected to peace and purity. See, as its name is a white witch, I wouldn’t see how the spirituality of this moth is positive, but because of the color and most cultures seeing that moths are part of the spirit world because they are mainly active at night. In literature, the symbolism of this moth is rebirth and transformation. In some cultures, people see that white moths represent how you are the light in other people’s lives or of those you have met. Spiritually, these moths are very looked upon as positive omens, but in superstitions and culture, they are looked upon as dark beings because, in some cultures, they see these moths as a signal of death. There is folklore among the Native Americans about the white witch moth that these moths bring news from their ancestors, which will give them a sense of safety.

Where are they found

This magnificent moth expands its region by going from the south of Mexico to North America and from Uruguay to Mexico. They like rainforests and temperature forests - Those are forests that stay the same temperature year-round- grasslands, rural areas and even cities. These beautiful insects happen to migrate a lot more than we think. When looking for a more common place to find these moths, they like to stay in Jalapa in Veracruz, Mexico. These moths are among the most adaptable because they migrate and go through so many different regions. The main places that these moths like to hang out are the woodlands and forest. These moths aren’t very migratory but they do tend to move around to different regions the observations of these moths have dated back 300 years making this moth known in South America and the southern parts of Texas.

What do they eat

These beautiful insects feed mostly on fruits, flowers, and shrubs. These moths also assist with pollination because they feed off the nectar of the slowers.
